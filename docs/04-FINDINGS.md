# Key Findings: What the Data Reveals

## Finding 1: Dramatic Escalation of Threat Intensity

### The Data

| Year | Total Alerts | Monthly Avg | Change | Status |
|------|------------|-----------|--------|--------|
| 2022 | 34,986 | 3,499 | — | Baseline |
| 2023 | 34,724 | 2,894 | -0.7% | Stable |
| 2024 | 52,270 | 4,356 | +50.5% | 🟡 Escalation |
| 2025 | 91,878 | 7,657 | +75.8% | 🔴 Crisis |
| 2026 | 59,416* | 9,903* | -35.3% | (6 months only) |

*2026 data is only 6 months; annualized pace would be ~120,000/year

### Key Insight
**The threat intensity more than doubled between 2023 and 2025.**

- **2023 vs 2024:** 50% increase suggests shift in military strategy
- **2024 vs 2025:** 76% increase signals major escalation
- **2026 Pace:** Continuing at record levels despite 2025 being the worst year

### What This Means
The data shows a clear escalation pattern:
1. **2022-2023:** Conflict settles into sustained pattern (~35K alerts/year)
2. **2024:** Significant increase begins (+50%)
3. **2025:** Most dangerous year (+76% from 2024, +165% from 2023)
4. **2026:** Continuing at unsustainable levels

### For Civilians
This escalation means:
- **Daily Impact:** In 2023, ~95 alerts/day. In 2025, ~252 alerts/day
- **Continuous Threat:** Going from routine (though serious) to crisis state
- **Infrastructure Stress:** Shelters, early warning systems, and civil defense stretched thin
- **Psychological Impact:** Sustained exposure to extreme threat levels

### Key Insight
**Western regions (Lviv, Ivano-Frankivsk, Zakarpattia) experienced unprecedented escalation in the last 6 months. The threat zone is expanding westward.**

### Critical Finding
These western regions:
- **Were Safer:** Historical alerts only 138-163 per 6-month period
- **Now Threatened:** Jumped to 457-559 alerts in recent 6 months
- **Rapid Change:** 3.3-3.4x increase in just 180 days
- **Expanding Threat:** Suggests front lines or threat range expanding

### Implications
1. **No Safe Haven:** Regions previously thought safer now face serious threat
2. **Expanding Conflict:** Threat is no longer limited to eastern regions
3. **Rapid Escalation:** Situation deteriorated significantly in late 2025/early 2026
4. **Resource Strain:** Western regions unprepared for this level of threat

### For Civilians
- Residents of western Ukraine who felt safer are now facing crisis-level threat
- Civil infrastructure in west not designed for this alert volume
- Psychological impact of sudden escalation

## Finding 4: Sustained High Impact & Duration

### Scale of Daily Threats

| Metric | Value | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Days with Alerts** | 1,563 | Nearly 4.3 years of continuous threat |
| **Average Daily Alerts** | 152 | Every single day, ~152 air raid alerts |
| **Median Daily Alerts** | 104 | Typical day has 100+ alerts |
| **Peak Single Day** | 1,004 | 1,004 alerts in one day (March 2025) |
| **Calm Days** | Rare | <50 alerts only in early conflict (2022) |

### Alert Duration by Region

| Region | Avg Duration | Interpretation |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| Dnipropetrovska | 218 minutes (3.6 hours) | Sustained operations |
| Donetska | 207 minutes (3.4 hours) | Prolonged threat |
| Kharkivska | 186 minutes (3.1 hours) | Extended alert period |
| Sumska | 132 minutes (2.2 hours) | Moderate duration |
| Poltavska | 80 minutes (1.3 hours) | Quick strikes |
| **Average** | **100-150 min** | **1.5-2.5 hours typical** |

### Key Insight
**Alerts aren't brief moments—they're sustained events lasting 80+ minutes on average.**

### What This Means
- **Disruption:** Each alert disrupts 1.5-3.6 hours of daily activity
- **Repetition:** With 150+ alerts per day, 24/7 disruption
- **Infrastructure:** Long durations mean sustained stress on shelters, power grids
- **Work/School:** Essentially impossible to maintain normal operations

### Psychological Impact
Living with:
- **Constant Threat:** 1,563 days of alerts (4+ years)
- **Frequent Disruption:** 150+ interruptions daily
- **Sustained Events:** Hours-long alert periods regularly
- **Unending Cycle:** No "all clear" period for recovery

## Finding 5: Patterns & Seasonality

### Monthly Distribution (When Danger Is Highest)

```
Most Dangerous Months: March, April, May, June
Moderate Months: October, November, December
Historically Quieter: January, February, July, August
```

### Quarterly Trends
- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Escalation from winter lows to spring highs
- **Q2 (Apr-Jun):** Consistently high (spring offensive season)
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep):** Variable, some reduction
- **Q4 (Oct-Dec):** Generally elevated but below spring peaks

### Key Insight
**There is mild seasonal variation, but no month is truly "safe."**
