## Critical Zone: Top 3 Regions

### Dnipropetrovska Oblast
- **Total Alerts:** 42,252 (17.8% of national total)
- **Daily Average:** 27 alerts/day
- **Peak Day:** 382 alerts in single day
- **Average Duration:** 218 minutes (3.6 hours per alert)
- **Status:** Most heavily affected region

**Strategic Importance:** Contains critical infrastructure; major industrial hub; strategically positioned in central Ukraine.

### Kharkivska Oblast
- **Total Alerts:** 35,325 (14.9% of national total)
- **Daily Average:** 23 alerts/day
- **Peak Day:** 356 alerts
- **Average Duration:** 186 minutes (3.1 hours)
- **Status:** Second most affected region

**Strategic Importance:** Near Russian border; major eastern city; strategic military location.

### Donetska Oblast
- **Total Alerts:** 26,735 (11.2% of national total)
- **Daily Average:** 17 alerts/day
- **Peak Day:** 298 alerts
- **Average Duration:** 207 minutes (3.4 hours)
- **Status:** Third most affected region

**Strategic Importance:** Active combat zone; major industrial region; critical to conflict.

### Concentration Impact
These 3 regions = 43.9% of national alerts
- Dnipropetrovska: 17.8%
- Kharkivska: 14.9%
- Donetska: 11.2%

**Implication:** While all regions affected, burden is disproportionately on these three.

## Eastern vs. Central vs. Western Regions

### Eastern Regions (Border Regions)
**Dnipropetrovska, Kharkivska, Donetska, Zaporizka, Sumska, Luhansk**
- Average alerts/region: 15-27 per day
- Status: Continuous, heavy threat
- Duration: Longest (180-220 min average)
- Assessment: Primary conflict zone

### Central Regions
**Poltavska, Mykolaivska, Chernihivska, Khersonska, Odeska, Kyivska, Cherkaska**
- Average alerts/region: 7-12 per day
- Status: Regular threat, significant
- Duration: Moderate (70-95 min average)
- Assessment: Secondary impact zone

### Western Regions
**Lvivska, Ivano-Frankivska, Zakarpattia, Volynska, Rivnenska, Ternopilska, Chernivtsi, Khmelnytska, Vinnytska, Zhytomyrska**
- Traditional level: 1-5 alerts/day
- **Recent Change:** Jumped to 10+ alerts/day
- **Assessment:** CRITICAL EXPANSION — threat zone expanding westward

### Key Finding
**Western regions that were historically safer are now facing 3-3.4x escalation.**

This is significant because:
- These regions lack infrastructure designed for such threat levels
- Populations were less prepared for air defense
- Civil defense systems may be inadequate
- Psychological impact of sudden threat increase

## Alert Duration: What It Tells Us

### Longest Average Durations
- **Dnipropetrovska:** 218 min (sustained operations)
- **Donetska:** 207 min (active combat zone)
- **Chernihivska:** 140 min (regular operations)

### Shortest Average Durations
- **Poltavska:** 80 min (quick, targeted strikes)
- **Mykolaivska:** 66 min (brief interventions)
- **Khersonska:** 71 min (limited scope)

**Interpretation:**
- Longer durations suggest sustained military operations nearby
- Shorter durations suggest incoming threats originating from far away
- Geographic location correlates with duration patterns

## Regional Vulnerability Index

### Most Vulnerable (Least Prepared)
1. **Lvivska, Ivano-Frankivska, Zakarpattia** — Recent escalation without historical infrastructure
2. **Kharkivska, Donetska** — Sustained crisis despite preparations
3. **Dnipropetrovska** — Overwhelming volume (42K alerts)

### Moderately Affected But Stable
- Central regions with established infrastructure
- Manageable daily alert volumes (7-12/day)
- Infrastructure somewhat adapted to threat

### Least Affected
- Far western regions with minimal historical alerts
- BUT: Recently escalating (3.3-3.4x increase)
- Least prepared for rapid escalation

## Regional Resilience Factors

### Factors Indicating Resilience
- Established civil defense infrastructure
- Experienced population (multi-year alerts)
- Industrial/economic capacity to rebuild
- Geographic distance from combat

### Vulnerability Factors
- Unprepared/new to threat (western regions)
- Overwhelming volume of alerts
- Long durations indicating sustained threat
- Close to military operations

## What This Means for Policy

### Resource Allocation Should Consider
1. **Ongoing Crisis:** Eastern regions need sustained support
2. **Emerging Crisis:** Western regions need rapid infrastructure deployment
3. **Unequal Burden:** Top 3 regions carrying 44% of national burden
4. **Escalating Danger:** Situation worsening, not improving

### Regional Priorities
1. **Immediate:** Support overwhelmed regions (Dnipropetrovska, Kharkivska)
2. **Urgent:** Prepare western regions for sustained threat
3. **Medium-term:** Build redundant systems in all regions
4. **Long-term:** Resilience infrastructure nationwide
